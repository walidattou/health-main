Understanding Your Blood Test Results: Complete Guide

Published on April 26, 2025 | By Dr. Marie Laurent | Category: Medical Tests

Blood tests are a fundamental tool for assessing your health, but their results can seem complex to understand. This guide will help you interpret key blood markers and better understand their significance for your health.

Whether you've had a routine blood test or a comprehensive check-up, we explain everything you need to know to understand your results.

The Basics of Blood Tests

A blood test evaluates many aspects of your health:

  • The state of your blood cells
  • The functioning of your organs
  • Your metabolism
  • Your immune system
  • Your nutrient levels

Good to Know

Blood test results can vary depending on the time of day, your recent diet, or your physical activity level. This is why fasting is often recommended for certain tests.

Complete Blood Count (CBC)

The CBC, or complete blood count, is the most common analysis. It examines:

Red Blood Cells

  • Red Blood Cells: transport oxygen
  • Hemoglobin: protein carrying oxygen
  • Hematocrit: volume occupied by red blood cells

White Blood Cells

  • Neutrophils: fight infections
  • Lymphocytes: specific immunity
  • Monocytes: immune defense

Platelets

Essential for blood clotting, their count can indicate:

  • Bleeding risks if too low
  • Thrombosis risks if too high

Liver Panel

This panel evaluates liver health through several enzymes:

Key Markers

  • AST/ALT: liver enzymes
  • γGT: gamma-glutamyl transferase
  • Alkaline Phosphatases
  • Bilirubin: bile pigment

Warning

Elevated liver enzymes can be caused by many factors, including alcohol consumption, certain medications, or a viral infection.

Lipid Panel

The lipid panel measures different types of cholesterol and triglycerides:

Analyzed Components

  • Total Cholesterol
  • HDL ("good" cholesterol)
  • LDL ("bad" cholesterol)
  • Triglycerides

Health Tip

A balanced diet, rich in fiber and unsaturated fatty acids, helps maintain a good lipid profile.

Electrolyte Panel

The electrolyte panel measures electrolytes in the blood:

Key Electrolytes

  • Sodium (Na+): water balance
  • Potassium (K+): muscle and heart function
  • Chloride (Cl-): acid-base balance
  • Calcium (Ca++): bones and clotting

Glucose Levels and HbA1c

These tests evaluate your glucose metabolism:

Fasting Glucose

  • Normal: 0.7-1.1 g/L
  • Pre-diabetes: 1.1-1.26 g/L
  • Diabetes: > 1.26 g/L

HbA1c (glycated hemoglobin)

Reflects your average glucose level over the past 3 months:

  • Normal: < 5.7%
  • Pre-diabetes: 5.7-6.4%
  • Diabetes: ≥ 6.5%

Understanding Normal Values

"Normal values" can vary depending on:

  • Gender
  • Age
  • The analysis laboratory
  • The analysis method used

Important

Reference values are always indicated on your test report. A slightly abnormal value does not necessarily mean a health problem.

What to Do with Abnormal Results

Steps to Follow

  1. Don't panic - an isolated abnormal value is not necessarily serious
  2. Consult your doctor to interpret the results
  3. Schedule follow-up tests if necessary
  4. Follow medical recommendations

Warning

Never change your treatment or lifestyle solely based on test results without your doctor's advice.
