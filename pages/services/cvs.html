Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about CVS Pharmacy services.

How do I transfer my prescriptions to CVS?

You can transfer your prescriptions to CVS in several ways: online via the CVS website or app, by phone by calling your local CVS pharmacy, or in person by bringing your current medication bottles or your current pharmacy's information. The process is usually quick, and in most cases, your medications will be ready for pickup the same day or the next day.

How does the medication delivery service work?

CVS offers several medication delivery options. Standard delivery is usually free, and medications are delivered within 1-2 days. Same-day delivery is available in many locations for an additional fee. To use this service, you can select the delivery option when renewing your prescription via the app or website, or request delivery by phone. CarePass members enjoy free delivery on all prescriptions and online orders.

Do I need an appointment to use MinuteClinic?

While MinuteClinic accepts walk-in patients, it is recommended to make an appointment online or via the CVS app to reduce wait times. You can see available times in real-time and book a slot that works for you. Some specific services, such as full physical exams or wellness visits, may require a prior appointment. For urgent issues, the walk-in service is always available during operating hours.

How do ExtraCare and ExtraBucks rewards work?

The ExtraCare program allows you to earn 2% cashback in ExtraBucks on almost all your in-store and online purchases. These ExtraBucks are like CVS money that you can use on your next purchases. They are issued quarterly, but you can choose to receive them after each transaction via the CVS app. Additionally, you receive personalized offers based on your shopping habits, exclusive weekly promotions, and a birthday reward. To take advantage, simply scan your ExtraCare card or enter your phone number during your purchases.

What insurance plans are accepted by CVS?

CVS Pharmacy accepts most major health insurance and prescription drug plans. This includes Medicare Part D, Medicaid, and many private and employer-provided insurances. For MinuteClinic services, many insurance plans are also accepted, with copayments often similar to those of a primary care physician visit. To check if your specific insurance is accepted, you can visit the CVS website, call your local pharmacy, or contact your insurer directly. If you don't have insurance, CVS offers several savings programs like the HealthSavings Pass to help reduce your costs.

How do I renew my prescriptions automatically?

CVS offers an automatic renewal service called ReadyFill®. To activate it, you can request it from your pharmacist, set it up via your online account or on the CVS app. Once activated, CVS will automatically prepare your renewals before you run out of medication. You will receive a notification when your medication is ready for pickup or delivery. This service is especially useful for medications you take regularly. You can manage your preferences, such as notification method and delivery mode, at any time via your account.
