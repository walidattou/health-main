Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about Walgreens services.

How do I transfer my prescriptions to Walgreens?

There are several ways to transfer your prescriptions to Walgreens:

  • Online: Create an account on the Walgreens website or via the mobile app, then follow the instructions to transfer your prescriptions
  • By phone: Call your local Walgreens pharmacy and provide the information from your current pharmacy
  • In person: Bring your current medication bottle to any Walgreens pharmacy

You will need information about your medication, your doctor, and your current pharmacy. The process usually takes 24 to 48 hours.

How does prescription medication delivery work?

Walgreens offers several delivery options for your prescription medications:

  • Standard delivery: Typically within 3-5 business days, often free for eligible orders
  • Express delivery: Same-day or next-day delivery in many areas, for an additional fee
  • Recurring delivery: Schedule automatic deliveries for your regular medications

You can set up delivery via the mobile app, website, or by calling your local pharmacy. Medications requiring refrigeration are delivered in special packaging to maintain the appropriate temperature.

How do myWalgreens reward points work?

With the myWalgreens program, you earn:

  • 1% in rewards on eligible in-store and online purchases
  • 5% on Walgreens brand products
  • Additional points during special promotions or wellness events

Points are automatically added to your account after each purchase. To use them, simply mention that you want to use your rewards during your next purchase or select them during online checkout. 1,000 points equal $1 in rewards, and points expire after 12 months of inactivity.

Can I get my vaccines at Walgreens?

Yes, Walgreens offers many vaccination services administered by certified pharmacists. Available vaccines include:

  • Seasonal flu vaccine
  • COVID-19 vaccines
  • Pneumonia
  • Shingles
  • Tetanus
  • Hepatitis A and B
  • And many more

You can schedule an appointment online, via the mobile app, or by phone. In some cases, walk-in vaccinations are also available. Most insurance plans cover the cost of common vaccines, and Walgreens accepts many insurance plans.

How can I access my prescriptions while traveling?

If you are traveling and need access to your medications, Walgreens offers several options:

  • With Walgreens' national network, you can access your prescriptions from any Walgreens pharmacy in the United States
  • Use the mobile app to temporarily transfer your prescription to a pharmacy near your destination
  • Ask your pharmacist to prepare a supply for the duration of your trip in advance
  • For international travel, consult your pharmacist who can provide a list of your medications and the necessary documents for traveling with prescription medications

It is recommended to plan ahead, especially for extended or international trips.

How does the Prescription Savings Club program work?

The Walgreens Prescription Savings Club is a paid membership program that offers discounts on prescription medications and other benefits:

  • Membership costs $20 per year for an individual or $35 per year for a family (including spouse, dependent children up to 23 years old, and pets)
  • The program offers discounted prices on over 8,000 medications, including generics starting at $5 for a 30-day supply
  • Discounts apply to medications not covered by insurance or for individuals without insurance
  • Members also receive 10% off Walgreens health services, vaccinations, and certain Walgreens brand products

You can sign up online, via the mobile app, or in-store. This program is particularly beneficial for individuals without prescription drug insurance or whose insurance does not cover certain medications.
