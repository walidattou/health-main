Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about SmileDirectClub.

Is SmileDirectClub suitable for everyone?

SmileDirectClub is ideal for mild to moderate cases of dental misalignment, such as gaps between teeth, crowded teeth, or slightly overlapping teeth. However, it is not recommended for complex orthodontic cases requiring extractions, surgery, or significant dental movements. An initial assessment will determine if you are a good candidate.

Is the treatment painful?

As with any orthodontic treatment, you may experience slight discomfort or pressure during the first few days of each new aligner, which is normal as your teeth move. This sensation is usually described as pressure rather than pain and subsides after a few days.

How long should I wear the aligners each day?

For optimal results, it is recommended to wear your aligners 20 to 22 hours per day. You should only remove them for eating, drinking (except water), and for your daily oral hygiene routine.

How does remote monitoring work?

Monitoring is primarily done through the SmileDirectClub mobile app. You take photos of your progress according to a set schedule and upload them to the app. A dentist or orthodontist reviews these photos and provides feedback. You can also contact the dental care team 24/7 by phone, chat, or video if you have questions or concerns.

What happens at the end of the treatment?

At the end of your treatment, you will receive a retainer to wear at night to maintain your results. The first set of retainers is included in the cost of your treatment. SmileDirectClub also offers a lifetime smile guarantee, meaning that if your teeth shift again in the future, you may be eligible for additional aligners at no cost (under certain conditions).

Is the treatment covered by insurance or a health plan?

Some health plans partially reimburse SmileDirectClub treatment, typically up to €500 depending on your contract. SmileDirectClub can provide the necessary documents for your reimbursement request, but it is recommended to check directly with your insurance before starting the treatment.
