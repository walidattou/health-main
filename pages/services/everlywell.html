Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to the most common questions about Everlywell's services.

Are Everlywell tests reliable?

Yes, all Everlywell tests are clinically validated and analyzed in CLIA-certified laboratories that meet the same standards used by doctors. Results are reviewed by independent physicians before being made available to you.

Are the tests covered by health insurance?

Currently, most Everlywell tests are not directly reimbursed by health insurance or social security. However, some tests may be eligible for reimbursement through certain health savings accounts (FSA/HSA) depending on your country. We recommend contacting your insurer for more information.

How are the results presented?

Results are presented in a secure digital dashboard with clear explanations of your values, reference ranges to understand where you stand, and information on possible actions you might consider based on your results. In some cases, personalized recommendations are provided.

How long does it take to receive results?

Once your sample is received by the lab, results are typically available within 5 to 7 business days. You will receive an email notification when your results are ready to be viewed in your online account.

Is it difficult to collect samples yourself?

Everlywell kits are designed to be easy to use, even without medical experience. Each kit contains detailed step-by-step instructions, and customer service is available to assist you if you have questions. Collection methods vary by test (finger prick, swab, saliva, or urine sample), but are all designed to be simple and painless.

What should I do if my results are abnormal?

If your results are abnormal, Everlywell will provide information on the meaning of these results and recommended next steps. For some tests, you may have the option to discuss your results with a healthcare professional. In all cases, we recommend sharing your results with your primary care physician, especially if abnormalities are detected.
